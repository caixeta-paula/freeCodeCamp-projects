ELON MUSK

A Tribute

From a software engineering student to a billionaire inventor...

  • 1971- Born in Pretoria, South Africa
  • 1983 - Teaches himself computer programming and sells his first video game for $500, at the age of 12
  • 1989 - Moves to Canada to attend Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario for undergraduate study, after obtaining citizenship from his Canadian-born mother
  • 1992 - Transfers to the University of Pennsylvania
  • 1995 - Moves to California to begin his PhD studies in applied physics and materials science at Stanford University, but abandons the program after a couple of days to pursue his entrepreneurialism
    - Starts a web software company with his brother (called Zip2) using $2,000 of their father's money, that serves as an Internet "city guide" for the newspaper publishing industry
  • 1997 - Receives a Bachelor of Science degree in physics as well as a Bachelor of Science degree in economics from UPenn
  • 1999 - Zip2 is acquired by Compaq for $307 million in cash and $34 million in stock options; Musk's 7% share in the company yielded $22 million
    - Founded X.com, an online financial services and email payment company, using $10 million from the sale of Zip2
  • 2000 - X.com merged with Confinity, which had a money transfer service called PayPal.
    - Musk is ousted from his role as CEO, primarily over his desire to move PayPal's Unix-based system to Microsoft Windows.
  • 2001 - The merged company focused on the PayPal service and was renamed PayPal.
  • 2002 - eBay acquired PayPal for $1.5 billion in stock, of which Musk received $165 million.
    - Musk uses $100 million of his fortune to found SpaceX, which develops and manufactures space launch vehicles with a focus on advancing the state of rocket technology (he is the CEO and CTO of the company to this day).
  • 2004 - Musk takes an active role in the Tesla Motors company by leading the Series A round of investment and joining the board of directors
  • 2006 - SpaceX is awarded a contract from NASA to continue the development and test of the company's launch vehicles and spacecraft in order to transport cargo to the ISS.
    - Musk provided the initial concept and capital for SolarCity, which is now the second largest provider of solar power systems in the United States.
  • 2008 - Musk assumed leadership of
    Tesla Motors as CEO and product architect, and released their first electric sports car, the Tesla Roadster
  • 2012 - Tesla begins the delivery of their four-door Model S sedan, and unveiled the Model X
  • 2013 - Musk unveils a concept for a high-speed transportation system with reduced-pressure tubes in which pressurized capsules ride on an air cushion driven by air compressors and linear induction motors
  • 2014 - Musk announces that Tesla will allow its technology patents to be used by anyone in good faith, to allow automobile manufacturers to speed up the development of electric cars.
  • 2015 - Musk announces a design competition, sponsored by SpaceX, for students and others to design Hyperloop pods
    - He also announced the creation of a not-for-profit artifical intelligence research company, OpenAI
  • 2016 - Musk co-founds a neurotechnology startup company called Neuralink, aiming to integrate the human brain with artificial intelligence.
  • 2017 - The plans for a new venture in relation to the Hyperloop, are set in motion as Musk begins digging a tunnel on the SpaceX campus. It was named The Boring Company.

"If something is important enough you should try, even if the probable outcome is failure."

Elon Musk, in an interview about investing in Tesla Motors
